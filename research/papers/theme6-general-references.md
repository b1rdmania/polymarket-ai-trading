## 1. Prediction Market (Wikipedia)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Type** | Encyclopedia entry |
| **Link** | [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prediction_market) |

### Why It's Useful
Not a paper, but the article usefully cites foundational work:
- **Francis Galton** — Original "wisdom of crowds" ox-weight experiment (1906)
- **Friedrich Hayek** — Dispersed knowledge argument (1945)
- **Wolfers & Zitzewitz** — "Interpreting Prediction Market Prices as Probabilities"
- **James Surowiecki** — *The Wisdom of Crowds* (2004)
- Empirical work on presidential elections

Good for tracking down older foundational pieces and getting oriented quickly.

## Foundational Works Referenced

These are frequently cited across the literature — worth having on your radar:

| Work | Author(s) | Year | Key Contribution |
|------|-----------|------|------------------|
| The Wisdom of Crowds | James Surowiecki | 2004 | Popular book establishing crowd aggregation thesis |
| The Use of Knowledge in Society | F.A. Hayek | 1945 | Theoretical foundation for dispersed knowledge |
| Interpreting Prediction Market Prices as Probabilities | Wolfers & Zitzewitz | 2006 | How to read market prices |

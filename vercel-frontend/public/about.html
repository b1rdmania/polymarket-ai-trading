MEAN REVERSION IN PREDICTION MARKETS

Dashboard Signals AI Insights Quality Resolution Trading Models Strategy Research Theory
Core thesis: Prediction markets show systematic emotional volatility. This creates exploitable arbitrage through mean reversion.

Approach

Prediction markets are dominated by retail traders who make decisions based on emotion and recent events rather than statistics. This creates predictable patterns of overreaction and subsequent correction.

Mechanism

When news breaks or sentiment shifts rapidly:

  • Positive news drives buying, prices spike above fair value
  • Negative news drives selling, prices crash below fair value
  • Time passes, emotions fade, prices revert to fundamentals

Example: A candidate makes a controversial statement in a debate. Their election probability drops from 65% to 48% in minutes. Historical data shows debate performance has minimal correlation with election outcomes. Within 48 hours, the price reverts to 62%. That 14-point swing is pure emotion.

Why This Works

1. Behavioral Finance

Decades of research show retail traders consistently:

  • Overreact to recent information
  • Overvalue extreme probabilities
  • Let partisan beliefs distort probability assessments
  • Chase momentum and create self-reinforcing spirals

2. Market Structure

  • No professional market makers to arbitrage away inefficiencies
  • Retail-dominated participant base (86.4% lose money on Polymarket)
  • Low capital requirements prevent large institutions from entering
  • Political markets especially irrational due to partisan emotions

3. Mean Reversion at Specific Horizons

Berg and Rietz (2018) found that prediction market mispricing mean-reverts most strongly at 7-21 day horizons. This is our target range.

What We Avoid

This is not:

  • Trying to predict election outcomes better than polls
  • Fundamental analysis or political forecasting
  • Long-term position taking
  • Following "smart money" or whale traders

This is:

  • Statistical arbitrage of emotional volatility
  • Systematic mean reversion trading
  • Fading crowd psychology at extremes
  • Risk-managed position sizing using Kelly Criterion

Validation

We are running all three models in paper trading mode for 30 days to:

  • Validate the strategy works in live markets
  • Determine which risk profile performs best
  • Measure real-world slippage and execution costs
  • Record live market data for future strategy improvements

After 30 days, we select the winning model and potentially deploy with real capital. The data decides which parameters work best.

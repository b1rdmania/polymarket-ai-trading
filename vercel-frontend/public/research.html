How It Works

These models trade on mean reversion: when market prices move too far from their true value, they snap back. This happens because of human biases.

Core Strategy

Mean Reversion

Markets overreact to news. Prices swing too high or too low. When they do, we bet on them returning to normal. Berg & Rietz (2018) showed this works in prediction markets for 40 years.

Position Sizing

Use Kelly Criterion. Bet more when confidence is high. Bet less when unsure. Never risk more than the math says.

Three Models

  • Conservative: High confidence only. Small positions.
  • Moderate: Balanced approach. Medium positions.
  • Aggressive: More trades. Larger positions.

Key Research

Berg & Rietz (2018)

"Longshots, Overconfidence, and Efficiency in the NCAA Tournament Betting Market"

Found that longshots (unlikely outcomes) are overpriced. People overestimate small probabilities. This creates profit opportunities.

Munger's Cognitive Biases

Charlie Munger identified 25 biases that affect trading:

  • Recency bias: People overweight recent events
  • Availability bias: Memorable events seem more likely
  • Confirmation bias: People seek evidence that confirms their views

These biases create mispricings. Models exploit them.

Kelly Criterion (1956)

Mathematical formula for position sizing. Maximizes long-term growth. Prevents ruin from bad trades.

f* = (bp - q) / b

where b = odds, p = win probability, q = loss probability
